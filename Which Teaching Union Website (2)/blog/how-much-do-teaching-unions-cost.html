How much do teaching unions cost? A 2025/26 comparison.

26 May 2026 · 9 min read

The short answer

  • A full-time classroom teacher pays roughly £170–£275 a year for full union membership across the major UK unions.
  • ECTs in year 1 pay just £1 with most unions. Trainees are free.
  • Support staff pay less than teachers — typically £10–£15 a month.
  • Leaders pay more than classroom teachers — typically £200–£500/yr depending on role.
  • Many unions add a local branch sub on top. Some are banded by salary. Some charge calendar-year, others academic year.

Teaching union fees aren't straightforward — they vary by role, by FTE, by salary band, by nation, sometimes by local council, and they change every year. Here's a clear-eyed look at what each major UK option costs in 2025/26.

Full-time classroom teacher (England & Wales)

If you're a full-time classroom teacher past your ECT years, these are the ballpark annual figures for the main options:

OrganisationAnnual cost (FT teacher)Notes
NEU£239–£274/yrIncluding 5–20% local branch sub
NASUWT£242/yrNo local subs
Edapt£194/yr (£17.95/mo)Not a trade union; ~10% saving if paid annually

The three are close enough in price that cost alone shouldn't be the deciding factor. What you get for the money — collective action, in-school reps, casework, political fund or not — varies more than the headline number.

Trainees, ECTs and probationers

Worth knowing: almost every option is free during initial teacher training, and most charge just £1 in your first year post-qualification. There's no good reason not to have something in place during this period.

StageTypical cost
ITT trainee (PGCE / School Direct / etc)£0 with most unions
Year 1 post-qualification (ECT 1 / probationer)£1 (NEU, NASUWT) up to full rate at some
Year 2 (ECT 2)~⅓ of full rate (NASUWT); full rate at NEU
Year 3~⅔ of full rate at NASUWT; full elsewhere

The small print to watch out for

  • Local branch subs. NEU and EIS add a local sub on top of the national rate, which varies by council/branch — usually 5–20%. NASUWT and Edapt don't.
  • Salary banding. UNISON, Community, INTO, UTU and Unite all band subs by earnings. The lower your salary, the less you pay.
  • FTE pro-rata. Most options pro-rata fees for part-time, but Edapt and UCAC use tier bands (0.6–0.8 FTE is one tier; ≤0.5 another).
  • Calendar year vs academic year. Some unions charge on a calendar-year cycle (January–December), others on an academic year (September–August). Worth checking when you join.
  • Annual vs monthly billing. Edapt offers ~10% saving for annual payment. SSTA has different rates depending on whether you pay before 31 January.
  • Political fund opt-out. If you opt out of a political fund (where applicable), your sub goes down slightly. Worth doing if politics isn't part of why you joined.

Is the cheapest option the best?

Not necessarily. Edapt is the cheapest for most teachers (£194/yr full-time), but it's not a trade union — it can't call strikes or do collective bargaining, and there are no in-school reps. If those things matter to you, you'd be paying for something you don't want. The right comparison is value for what you actually want from membership, not just price.
